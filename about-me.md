<div class="card">
  <p>
    I am a <strong>PhD Candidate in Sociology</strong> at 
    <a href="https://www.concordia.ca/artsci/sociology-anthropology.html" target="_blank">Concordia University</a>, 
    a <strong>CAnD3 Doctoral Fellow</strong> at 
    <a href="https://www.mcgill.ca/cand3/our-people/fellows-2024-25" target="_blank">McGill University</a>, 
    and a <strong>CRDCN Emerging Scholar</strong> at the 
    <a href="https://crdcn.ca" target="_blank">Canadian Research Data Centre Network (CRDCN)</a>. My doctoral research focuses on the 
    <strong>social determinants of mental health</strong> and population-level <strong>disparities in mental health outcomes</strong>, particularly psychological distress, anxiety, and depression among <strong>youth</strong> and <strong>young adults</strong> in Canada. I use statistical modeling and machine learning to analyze these disparities and recommend evidence-based policies for improving mental health.
  </p>

  <p>
    My doctoral research is supported by 
    <a href="https://www.concordia.ca/artsci/sociology-anthropology.html" target="_blank">Concordia University</a>, 
    <a href="https://www.frq.gouv.qc.ca" target="_blank">FRQSC</a>, 
    the <a href="https://www.ciqss.org" target="_blank">QICSS</a>, and the 
    <a href="https://crdcn.ca" target="_blank">CRDCN</a>.
  </p>

  <p>
    I am passionate about <strong>quantitative research methods</strong>, <strong>survey methodology</strong>, 
    <strong>applied social statistics</strong>, and <strong>machine learning</strong>, particularly in the context of large-scale surveys with complex sampling designs.
  </p>
