  <h2>TEACHING PHILOSOPHY</h2>
  <div class="teaching-card">
    <p>
      <i class="fas fa-lightbulb icon"></i>
      I approach teaching as a way to expose students to new ideas, concepts, and methods of thinking about and engaging with social problems and inequalities. I view myself as a facilitator who guides students through the process of learning, applying knowledge, and developing practical skills.In my courses, I incorporate 
      <strong>experiential learning</strong> to provide students with more opportunities to work with real-world data to examine topics such as poverty, income inequality, food insecurity, housing, and disparities in health outcomes. 
    </p>
    <p>
      <i class="fas fa-hands-helping icon"></i>
      I believe this hands-on approach not only helps students connect theoretical concepts with practical analysis but also gives them a chance to see the impact their work can have 
      in creating a  functional and healthy society. I value slow and thoughtful learning and want students to engage with the concepts and 
      methods they are exposed to.
    </p>
